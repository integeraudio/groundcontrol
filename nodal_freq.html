Technical Notes

Each unit operates as a sealed-cavity, resistively-damped diaphragmatic absorber — a decoupled piston resonator where the air column provides the restoring spring and OC 705 rigid fibreboard controls the Q factor through viscous flow resistance. The Richlite membrane is mechanically decoupled from the Baltic Birch frame, suppressing plate-bending modes so the panel behaves as a rigid translating mass. All three units share a common 6.47" exterior depth and 24" × 29" Richlite face. Frequency tuning is achieved by varying membrane surface mass density (MLV addition for Nodal 40) and active cavity depth (false back for Nodal 70).
